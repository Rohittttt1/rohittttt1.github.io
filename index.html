Info on eagles

Adult eagles are dark brown, with white feathers covering their heads. They also have yellow beaks, large talons, which they use to grab prey. Eagles fly alone because they are solitary. They are not social type of birds unless they are mating. Eagles fly high up in the sky, searching for food.

How Eagles Hunt

The claws and feet of eagles are just the right size and shape for taking their prey. Eagles don't always swoop down on their prey at great speed. Instead, they may simply spread their wings wide and float slowly down - dropping out of the sky so silenly that the prey can't hear them coming.
